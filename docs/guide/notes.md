# Notes

Notes let you record anything that happened on a particular date that might affect your lab results or help you remember context later. They are completely independent of your lab entries — you do not need a blood draw on the same date to add a note.

## Adding a Note

Scroll to the **Data & Notes** section at the bottom of the dashboard and click **Add Note**. Enter:

- **Date** — the date the note applies to
- **Text** — what you want to remember

Notes can be anything: starting a new medication, a period of high stress, an illness, a dietary change, a travel period, or a doctor's comment you want to keep alongside your data.

## Notes on the Dashboard

Notes appear interleaved with your lab entries in the chronological timeline, displayed with a **yellow left border** to distinguish them from lab data at a glance.

## Yellow Dots on Charts

Open the **Layers** dropdown in the chart area and enable **Notes** to show a **yellow dot** on every chart at each date where you have a note. Hovering over a dot shows the note text as a tooltip.

This lets you visually correlate events with changes in your biomarkers. For example, you might see that a spike in CRP lines up with a note about an infection, or that your testosterone dipped during the week you noted extreme work stress.

::: tip
The note overlay is off by default. Turn it on in the Layers dropdown whenever you want to see how events correlated with your trends.
:::

## Notes in the Detail Modal

When you open the detail view for a specific marker, any notes from dates that appear on that chart are shown alongside the data — marked with a memo icon. This keeps relevant context visible without leaving the detail view.

## Notes in AI Context

All your notes are included when the AI builds context for the chat panel or for per-marker explanations. The AI can reference your notes directly — for example, explaining a CRP elevation in the context of a note you wrote about a cold that week.

## Editing and Deleting Notes

Click any note in the dashboard list to edit its text or date. Use the delete button to remove it. Deleting a note removes its dot from all charts immediately.

## Export and Import

Notes are included in your JSON export. When you import a file, duplicate notes (same date and text) are automatically removed.
