Why this exists

Because your doctor
has 7 minutes

You get your blood work back as a PDF. Some numbers are flagged. Your doctor glances at it, says “everything looks fine,” and moves on.

But nobody’s tracking your ferritin dropping for two years straight. Nobody’s connecting your fasting insulin to your sleep. Nobody asks about your light exposure, your water quality, your stress.

I built Lab Charts because health data shouldn’t live in a drawer. I wanted trends, not snapshots. AI that knows my full context — diet, sleep, exercise, environment, goals — not one that just compares numbers to ranges. And I wanted to own my data — stored on my terms, not on someone else’s server.

How it works

Three steps to clarity

From a stack of lab PDFs to actionable health insights in under a minute.

1
📄

Import

Drop any lab PDF — any format, any language. AI extracts every biomarker, scrubs personal info first. Batch-import a stack at once. Unknown markers are auto-created as custom markers.

2
📈

Explore

Interactive charts with trend detection, date range filtering, and chart layers — overlay supplement timelines, note markers, and cycle phase bands. Heatmaps, side-by-side comparisons, and a correlation viewer for cross-marker analysis.

3
🧠

Understand

AI analyzes your results in the context of diet, sleep, exercise, stress, cycle phase, and goals. Named chat threads persist across sessions. Click any marker for a quick AI breakdown.
